About

I'm graduating in 2026 with a predicted First Class Honours. My work sits at the intersection of mechanical design and medical technology — I'm drawn to problems where careful engineering makes a real, tangible difference to people.

Across my degree I've built a strong foundation in CAD and simulation-driven design, working through SOLIDWORKS, Autodesk Fusion, Ansys, and Zemax OpticStudio to take projects from concept to tested prototypes. My dissertation — a sub-£200 smartphone spectrometer for optical diagnostics — let me own the housing and optical-layout workstream end to end. Alongside coursework, I led the powertrain department for Queen Mary Formula Student.

I'm now looking for opportunities in mechanical, automotive, and medical-device engineering. I speak Arabic, English, French, and some German, and I'm relocating to Munich in late 2026 for my MSc.
