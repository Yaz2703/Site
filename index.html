About

I'm an engineering student at QMUL, predicted to graduate in 2026 with a First Class Honours. My involvement in work in both the mechanical and medical areas has given me a unique set of interdisciplinary skills, making me a versatile asset.

Across my degree I've built a strong foundation in CAD and simulation-driven design, working through SOLIDWORKS, Autodesk Fusion, Ansys, StarCCM+, and Zemax OpticStudio to take projects from conceptual designs to tested prototypes. My final year project, which was a sub-£200 smartphone spectrometer for optical diagnostics, allowed me to lead a full-scale project on developing a device which allows users to have a cheap yet capable optical analysis tool at their disposal.

Alongside my academics, I led the powertrain department for Queen Mary Formula Student for two years, and was a contributor at the Queen Mary Centre of Undergraduate Research.

I'm now looking for opportunities in mechanical, automotive, and medical-device engineering. I speak Arabic, English, French, and some German.
